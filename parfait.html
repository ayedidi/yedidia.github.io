PARFAIT!

Roles:

Game Overview

Parfait is a one-player VR parfait cafe game. The chef serves impatient customers while managing a lively kitchen.

Process

I designed this game while taking a Virtual Reality Specialization certificate course from Goldsmith's, University of London. While I build and design this for VR, I ultimately want to the game to be available on iOS and Android devices as well. The game is set in a parfait cafe that uses 3D models and animations of the chef (you), your work station, customers, ingredients, and your boss. The goal of the stakes in this game is to keep the player highly stimulated. The desired medium is the Oculus Quest HMD.

As fruits run around your workstation, you are to catch them, add them to orders, and serve the correct order to the customer, all while keeping the bitter berries from hopping in. The customers get demanding when they are hungry so it's imperative to protect orders from impatient customers who will slam their spoons into parfaits and give the cafe a bad review. The more you play, the more you get to know the regular characters and understand their needs.

The player uses the triggers on the Quest controller to pick up cups, utensils, and fruits. The player receives haptic feedback through vibration when an action incurs damage, like when a bitter berry hops into a parfait and ruins an order. They can smash items with button interactions the throw them as well.

The Three Illusions

This game is set in a cafe that changes appearance at different stages of gameplay. The player will feel as though they are in the place when they look around and see a bustling cafe. The player seeing an avatar's body when they look down contributes to the illusion of embodiment. As the player interacts with food and customers, they respond and the very fictional events will seem realistic, creating the illusion of plausibility.

Influence and Inspiration

My imagined game takes elements from Job Simulator, Cook-Out, and Takelings House Party. The game space is a kitchen based on the level the player has reached. Players can serve computer-generated customers based on their requests, like Job Simulator. Like Cook-Out, the game would ideally have multi-player modes and capabilities. Like Takelings House Party, there are tiny beings the player interacts with as part of the game.
