Game Overview

Parfait is a one-player VR parfait cafe game.

I designed this game while taking a Virtual Reality Specialization certificate course from Goldsmith's University of London.

Process

The game takes place at a parfait cafe that uses 3D models and animations of the chef (you), your work station, customers, ingredients, and your boss. The goal of the stakes in this game is to keep the player highly stimulated. The desired medium is the Oculus Quest HMD.

As fruits run around your workstation, you are to catch them, add them to the correct order, and serve the correct order to the correct customer, all while keeping the bitter berries from hopping in. Not only are you managing the food, but protecting orders from impatient customers who will slam their spoons into parfaits that are unfinished and give the cafe a bad review. The customers get demanding when they are hungry so it's imperative to watch out for those who aren't morning people until they've had their parfait. This allows the chef to get to know the regular characters and understand their needs.

The player uses the triggers on the quest controller to pick up cups, utensils, and fruits. The player receives haptic feedback through vibration when an action incurs damage, like when a bitter berry hops into a parfait and ruins an order. They can smash items with button interactions the throw them as well.

Influence and Inspiration

My imagined app takes elements from Job Simulator, Cook-Out, and Takelings House Party. The game space is a kitchen based on the level the player has reached. Players can serve computer-generated customers based on their requests, like Job Simulator. Like Cook-Out, the game would ideally have multi-player modes and capabilities. Like Takelings House Party, there are tiny beings the player interacts with as part of the game.
